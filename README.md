## 1. Hook: "What is a fish?"

The fun, mind-bending opener. The punchline: **"fish" is not a real scientific
category.**

- "Fish" is a **paraphyletic** group — it lumps together jawless fish, sharks
  (cartilaginous), and bony fish, but conveniently excludes everything that crawled
  onto land (tetrapods: amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals... us).
- A valid scientific group (a **clade / monophyletic group**) must include an
  ancestor *and all its descendants*. "Fish" leaves out its land-dwelling
  descendants, so it's not valid.
- The kicker: a **lungfish or coelacanth is more closely related to YOU** (a human)
  than it is to a salmon or a shark. Lobe-finned fish are on the same branch that
  led to four-limbed land animals.
- Stephen Jay Gould's line, paraphrased: there is no such thing as a fish.
- Tie back to the book: *Why Fish Don't Exist* uses this idea as a metaphor —
  the categories we cling to can be illusions, and letting go of them can be freeing.

**Sources:**

- [Why is there no such thing as a fish? (paraphyletic explainer)](https://scienceinsights.org/why-is-there-no-such-thing-as-a-fish/)

## 3. Fish Farming / Aquaculture

How we actually raise aquatic animals at scale, plus my interest in shrimp.

- Aquaculture now supplies a huge share of the seafood we eat; wild catch plateaued
  decades ago, so farming filled the gap.
- Main methods to contrast:
  - **Ponds** — earthen ponds, common for tilapia, catfish, prawns. Cheap, simple.
  - **Cages / net pens** — in open water (e.g. salmon). Higher output, but waste and
    escapes affect the surrounding environment.
  - **RAS (Recirculating Aquaculture Systems)** — closed-loop indoor tanks that
    filter and reuse water. Great biosecurity and water efficiency, but energy-
    hungry and can be a barren environment for the fish.
- **Shrimp angle** (my interest): freshwater prawn farming runs in three phases —
  hatchery → nursery → grow-out. At the hobby end, **Neocaridina davidi** (cherry
  shrimp) breed readily in a home tank, no special gear, females carry eggs and
  release fully-formed mini shrimp. A nice "farming at home" tie-in.

**Sources:**
- [Sustainable fish farming – WRI](https://www.wri.org/insights/sustainable-fish-farming-5-strategies-get-aquaculture-growth-right)
- [Pond vs cage aquaculture](https://thefishsite.com/articles/the-pros-and-cons-of-pond-vs-cage-aquaculture-in-kenya)
- [What is RAS aquaculture?](https://asc-aqua.org/blog/explained-what-is-ras-aquaculture/)

## 4. The Ethics of Fishing (my opinion)

The reflective section. Present the tensions, then share where I land — and invite
disagreement.

- **Overfishing & wild stocks** — capture fisheries have stagnated/declined; some
  species and migratory runs are collapsing.
- **Farming as a fix, with caveats** — reduces pressure on wild stocks but brings
  its own issues: feed (often made from wild fish), pollution, disease, escapes.
- **Welfare** — most farmed aquatic animals have little to no legal welfare
  protection; the question "is it cruel?" has no clean yes/no answer. Well-run,
  enrichment-focused operations can reduce suffering.

## 5. Fish Native to Boston / Massachusetts

Local hook — bring it home for the team.

- **Freshwater (Charles River & MA):** brook trout (the state's native trout),
  largemouth bass, yellow perch, pumpkinseed, golden shiner, black crappie, white
  catfish, carp.
- **Migratory / diadromous (the ecological stars):** alewife & blueback herring
  (river herring), American shad, American eel — these migrate between river and
  sea and are declining due to dams and climate change.
- **Saltwater / Boston Harbor:** striped bass (the iconic spring/summer run),
  bluefish, Atlantic cod offshore.
- Conservation note: dam removal and fish ladders on the Charles are helping
  migratory runs recover.

**Sources:**
- [Native fish species of Massachusetts](https://thecurrentangler.com/2021/11/29/ma-native-fish/)
- [Freshwater fishes of Massachusetts – Mass.gov](http://mass.gov/info-details/freshwater-fishes-of-massachusetts)

# Part 2: The Website

A single-page, **mobile-first** multiplayer fishing **game**. The team plays it
together on their phones during the session. Built later in a **separate Kiro
instance** — this project just documents *how* it will be built.

📄 **Full build spec: [website-plan.md](./website-plan.md)**

Highlights:
- Cheap custom domain (e.g. `fridaylunchfish.com`) deployed on **Vercel**, shared via
  a QR code.
- **One page, one game** — our own take on the **Stardew Valley fishing mechanic**,
  made real-time multiplayer with **[playhtml](https://playhtml.fun/)**. The loop:
  catch a fish, **name it**, and send it to a **shared aquarium** the whole team
  fills together in real time, with live presence showing who's fishing.
- Other interesting fish websites (e.g. Neal Agarwal's "The Deep Sea") are just
  **shown on the slides**, not embedded or rebuilt.

See the plan doc for the gameplay loop, rendering/library options, asset sources,
licensing checklist, and build order.
